## 0. The one-paragraph version

QuizRush's backend (Valkey data modeling, scoring, TTL, streams) is genuinely solid for a hackathon project. The problems are concentrated in three places: **(1) the quiz answers are shipped to the browser in plain text**, so anyone can open devtools and see every correct answer before playing — this is the single most damaging bug because it undermines the entire premise of "competitive quiz." **(2) refreshing the page or guessing a room code breaks the experience** — no session rehydration, score resets to zero on rejoin, and three GET endpoints leak any room's full state/PDF/leaderboard to anyone who can guess a 4-character code. **(3) the emotional climax of the product — the moment the quiz ends and a winner is crowned — is the flattest screen in the app**, with a broken icon, no confetti, and no way to play again. Everything else (the 1,316-line monolith component, the dead `lucide-react` dependency, the 18 `transition-all`s, zero `:active` press states) is real but secondary.
